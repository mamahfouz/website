Agent-based Modelling (ABM):
======
ABM is a bottom-up approach to building financial markets composed of various types of participants (hedge funds, banks, etc.). In these models, the agents are **reactive** to new unseen scenarios allowing them to perform counterfactual reasoning and generate different future paths. In my work, I use ABMs as a method to allow for improved backtesting of trading strategies.

Current backtesting algorithms used for evaluating the potential future performance of trading strategies suffer from the inability to accurately take into account the impact of the new trade introduced as part of the strategy. ABMs can potentially address this with a good calibration of the agent's parameters from real data.
